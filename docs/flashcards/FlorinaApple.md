## Tree Traits

### Canadian Hardiness Zone
- Zone 4  (Frost-tolerant and suitable for northern climates.)

### Soil Preference
- Sandy loam, loam, or clay loam. Prefers average to moist conditions 
with well-drained soil. Avoid planting anywhere that floods for more 
than two weeks in the spring.

### Flowering Time
- Mid to late season (bloom group 4)

### Bloom Colour
- White

### Pollination Requirements
- Not self-fertile. Requires a pollinator of a different apple variety 
that blooms around the same time. Diploid — provides good pollen for 
others.

### Sun/Shade Requirements
- Full sun (approx. 8–10 hours of sun daily)

### General Growth Habit
- Vigorous, spreading, tip-bearing tree. Precocious and a good annual 
cropper. Strong producer — each tree can yield approximately 60–70 kg 
of fruit per season. Fruit hangs well on the tree and is also a good 
choice for attracting wildlife.

### Overall Disease Resistance Rating
- Excellent. Very resistant to scab, resistant to fireblight, powdery
mildew, and rosy apple aphid. Somewhat susceptible to cedar-apple rust.
Highly suited to organic production.

---

## Fruit Traits

### Recommended Use
- Fresh eating, storage. Also used for jams and preserves.

### Fruit Size
- Medium to large (110–140g). 

### Storage
- Keeps well for approximately three months. Can keep into January or 
early February under good storage conditions.

### Harvest
- Early to mid October

## Fun Facts
- Although developed in France, all parent varieties are American — 
Jonathan, Golden Delicious, Rome Beauty, and *Malus floribunda* 
(for scab resistance).
- Also sold under the trademark name Querina®.

### History
- Developed at INRA in Beaucouzé, near Angers, France. Bred in the 
1970s, officially released in 1977. Became a popular commercial 
cultivar in European markets through the 1980s.

### Cultural Uses
- Dessert apple. Used in France for jams, jellies, and baked goods.
