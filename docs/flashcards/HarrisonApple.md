## Tree Traits

### Canadian Hardiness Zone
- Zone 5

### Soil Preference
- Sandy loam, loam, or clay loam. Prefers average to moist, well-drained
conditions. Avoid planting anywhere that floods for more than two weeks
in the spring.

### Flowering Time
- Middle

### Bloom Colour
- White

### Pollination Requirements
- Requires a pollinator of a different apple variety blooming at the
same time.

### Sun/Shade Requirements
- Full sun (approx. 8–10 hours of sun daily)

### General Growth Habit
- Vigorous and very productive. Upright, tip-bearing. Reliable annual
cropper. A single tree can reportedly produce approximately 100 bushels.
Needs thinning to maintain annual bearing.

### Overall Disease Resistance Rating
- Medium. Scab resistant. Resistant to rot.

## Fruit Traits

### Recommended Use
- Cider, fresh eating. One of few American cider apples suitable as
a single-variety cider. Can be eaten fresh though flesh is somewhat dry.

### Fruit Size
- Medium-small. Round, long-stemmed (also known as "Long Stem"). Yellow
skin with dark spots. Rich, yellow, firm flesh. Pleasant and sprightly
flavour but somewhat dry.

## Fun Facts
- Believed to have originated in the early 1700s, grown by a Mr. Osborne
in New Jersey — also known as "Osborne Apple" and "Long Stem."
- Was nearly lost to extinction — rediscovered in 1976 at an old cider
mill in Livingston, New Jersey just one week before the last known tree
was cut down.
- In 1817, William Coxe noted it commanded the highest price on the New
York market as a single-variety cider apple.

### Illustrations and/or Photos

### History
- First described by William Coxe in 1817 and widely grown in Essex
County, New Jersey during the 1800s. Fell into obscurity in the early
1900s due to the temperance movement and urbanization. Rediscovered in
1976 by a fruit variety collector and later propagated by pomologist Tom
Burford, allowing it to be grown again across North America.

### Cultural Uses
- Central to Newark, New Jersey's historic cider production alongside
Campfield and Graniwinkle. Prized for producing a high-coloured, rich,
strong single-variety cider. Now experiencing renewed popularity among
craft cider makers.
