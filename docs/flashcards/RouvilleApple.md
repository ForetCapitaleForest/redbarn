## Tree Traits

### Canadian Hardiness Zone
- Zone 3

### Soil Preference
- Sandy loam, loam, or clay loam. Prefers average to moist, well-drained
conditions. Avoid planting anywhere that floods for more than two weeks
in the spring.

### Flowering Time
- Middle

### Bloom Colour
- White

### Pollination Requirements
- Requires a pollinator of a different apple variety blooming at the
same time. Specific compatible varieties not documented — any
mid-season apple or crabapple nearby should suffice.

### Sun/Shade Requirements
- Full sun (approx. 8–10 hours of sun daily)

### General Growth Habit
- Vigorous, upright. Precocious — tends to bear fruit earlier than most
varieties. Good annual production.

### Overall Disease Resistance Rating
- Excellent. Immune to all five known North American races of apple scab.
Highly resistant to fireblight. Performs well in organic production.

---

## Fruit Traits

### Recommended Use
- Fresh eating, cooking, cider, juice, sauce. Bittersweet cider
classification — high sugar, high tannins, low acidity.

### Fruit Size
- Medium-large (7–8 cm). Oblate, ribbed. Red blush over pale
greenish-yellow skin. Coarse, juicy, pale cream flesh. Subacid.

### Storage
- Approximately 2 months in cool, humid conditions.

### Harvest
- August — Late

### Cider Class
- Class: Bittersweet
- Sugar: High, SG >1.060
- Acidity: Low, TA <4.5 g/L
- Tannins: High, >2.5 g/L
- Juice Yield: High

## Fun Facts
- A rare Canadian-bred cider apple with high sugar and tannin levels —
qualities more commonly associated with traditional English and French
cider apples.
- Part of the Quebec apple breeding program that also produced Blair
and Richelieu — all scab-immune cold-hardy cultivars.

### History
- Created in 1962 by R.L. Granger and G.L. Rousselle at the Agriculture
and Agri-Food Canada research centre in St-Jean-sur-Richelieu, Quebec.
Introduced in 1983. Part of a breeding program transferred from Ottawa
in 1971, focused on cold hardiness, scab resistance, and dessert and
cider quality for northern climates.

### Cultural Uses
- Grown as a multi-purpose apple in Quebec and across eastern Canada.
Valued by cider makers for its high sugar and tannin content. Produces
an aromatic jelly and is used in juice and sauce.
