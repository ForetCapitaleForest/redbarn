## Tree Traits

### Canadian Hardiness Zone
- Zone 4

### Soil Preference
- Sandy loam, loam, or clay loam. Prefers average to moist, well-drained 
conditions. Avoid planting anywhere that floods for more than two weeks 
in the spring.

### Flowering Time
- Mid-season

### Bloom Colour
- Pink

### Pollination Requirements
- Requires a pollinator of a different apple variety blooming at the 
same time.

### Sun/Shade Requirements
- Full sun (approx. 8–10 hours of sun daily)

### General Growth Habit
- Vigorous. Upright habit, spreads slightly with age. Heavy cropper.
Does well in organic orchards.

### Overall Disease Resistance Rating
- Unknown

---

## Fruit Traits

### Recommended Use
- Cooking, cider, jelly. Not well suited for fresh eating due to high 
acidity.

### Fruit Size
- Medium. Round. Yellow-green skin with a red blush. Distinctive pink 
and white marbled flesh.

### Storage
- Not yet well documented — to be confirmed

### Harvest
- Mid-late season

---

## Fun Facts
- The pink and white marbled flesh is highly distinctive and unusual 
among apple varieties.
- Despite its striking appearance, it is quite tart — described as 
making "one bite will make you pucker."
- Still considered a newer variety with limited documentation available.

### History
- Bred in Toronto, Ontario by Fred Jansen, one of the founders of the 
North American Fruit Explorers (NAFEX). A Canadian-bred cultivar still 
in its early years of wider distribution.

### Cultural Uses
- Valued for aromatic jelly, cider, and cooking rather than fresh eating.
