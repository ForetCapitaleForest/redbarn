## Tree Traits

### Canadian Hardiness Zone
- Zone 4

### Soil Preference
- Sandy loam, loam, or clay loam. Prefers average to moist, well-drained
conditions. Avoid planting anywhere that floods for more than two weeks
in the spring.

### Flowering Time
- Late

### Bloom Colour
- White

### Pollination Requirements
- Requires a pollinator of a different apple variety blooming at the
same time.

### Sun/Shade Requirements
- Full sun (approx. 8–10 hours of sun daily)

### General Growth Habit
- Moderately vigorous, spreading, precocious. Crops well but tends
toward biennial bearing.

### Overall Disease Resistance Rating
- Medium. Resistant to scab and brown rot. Susceptible to canker.

### Recommended Use
- Cider only.

### Storage
- Does not store well.

### Harvest
- October — Early

### Cider Class
- Class: Bittersweet
- Sugar: Medium, SG 1.054
- Acidity: Low, TA 1.8 g/L
- Tannins: High, 4.1 g/L
- Juice Yield: 275 mL/lb
- Taste: Full and aromatic
- Recommendations: Good in blends (particularly with Ellis Bitter) but can also be made into an excellent single-variety cider
  
*Information based on Claude Jolicoeur's The New Cidermaker's Handbook and Washington State University's "Cultivar Performance Gallery"

## Fun Facts
- One of the earlier-ripening bittersweet cider apples, making it
useful for extending the pressing season before later varieties are
ready.
- Juice is described as full and aromatic with medium sugar and high
tannins — pairs particularly well with Ellis Bitter in blends.

### Illustrations and/or Photos

### History
- Dates back to potentially before the 19th century. Traditionally
grown on farms in Devon and Somerset, England. Remains a traditional
English cider variety with limited documented history.

### Cultural Uses
- Traditional bittersweet cider apple from the West Country of
England. Used in both blended and single-variety ciders.
